About this game

Our research explores how emotion recognition games can help middle school students with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) improve their social-emotional skills. The objective is to provide an interactive, safe game that lets kids practice identifying emotions at their own speed. We hope to address the special needs of kids with ASD, who frequently benefit from routines and few distractions, by creating a game with repetitive gameplay and an interface. Our work is based on theories like Cognitive Load Theory, which helps break down learning into manageable steps, and Ecological Systems Theory, which considers the influences of family, school, and community.
